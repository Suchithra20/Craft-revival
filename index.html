What Kaaru is

Three things.
Inseparable.

A maker

Every piece is made by hand here in Dublin, using techniques learned directly from South Indian craft traditions. The making is the research.

Sungudi
tie-dye

Madurai, Tamil Nadu · GI Protected · Critically endangered

A resist-dyeing tradition producing geometric dot patterns on silk, historically made for temple offerings and festive wear. Practiced today by a handful of families in Madurai. Almost entirely unknown outside India.

No drawing. No painting. Pure process — the pattern emerges from how you fold, bind and dip the cloth in natural dye. Each piece is unrepeatable.

How we work

Every piece has a paper trail.

⌖

Learn from source

We don't learn from YouTube. We go to the makers, the communities, the institutions who carry this knowledge. If we can't do that, we don't make it.

◎

Document everything

From first attempt to finished piece, every step is photographed and written up. The documentation is published in the Journal — free, open, credited.

◇

Credit always

The tradition, the region, the community, the individual makers who taught us — all named, every time. On the object, on the website, in the workshop.

△

Make it new

We're not making replicas. The techniques are traditional; the objects are for life in Dublin today. Scarves, table linens, wall pieces, garments.
